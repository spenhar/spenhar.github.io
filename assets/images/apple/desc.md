The headquarters of Apple Inc., known as Apple Park, sits in Cupertino, California. Photographed here mid-construction via satellite, the circular form — a donut of glass, steel, and California scrubland — raises persistent questions about the relationship between corporate structure and spatial ideology.

At 2.8 million square feet, it houses roughly 12,000 employees. The ring shape was personally championed by Steve Jobs before his death. The surrounding terrain, in this image, is caught between states — vegetation patchy, the machinery of construction still visible.

What does it mean to build an infinite loop?
