"First light" is the term astronomers use for the first time a new telescope observes the sky. It is a rite of passage — the instrument's debut, its proof of function.

This image is the Gran Telescopio Canarias' first light, captured in 2007: a star near Polaris designated Tycho 1205081. Unremarkable in most respects — a dim point source, a position in a catalog — but historically significant as the moment a 10.4-meter mirror first opened its eye.

The image is grainy. The star is small. That's the point.
